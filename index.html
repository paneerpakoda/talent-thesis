The FrameHarsh D

First, the criteria.
Then the bet.

Where a designer should go in 2026 isn't a matter of prestige. Judge any role on five things — not the logo on the door.
Scale of impact
How many real people feel your decisions.
Team quality
Design leadership, researchers, peers to learn from.
Decision rights
Do you ship — or just hand off mockups?
Learning velocity
How fast you see real impact and feedback.
Org leverage
Real product power, not token inclusion.
This deck argues a bank can win on these — not that every bank does. Use the criteria; hold the conclusion lightly.

The Canvas · HonestyHarsh D

Banks own the rails. Apps own the screen. Both are true.

Pretending otherwise is how a deck loses a sharp audience. So let's be precise about where each kind of power actually sits.
Consumer-UX power
≈85%
of UPI volume runs through two non-bank apps — PhonePe 47.8% + Google Pay 37%. The flashy front-end isn't where banks win.
Infrastructure & trust power
The rails
Settlement, the licence, regulated trust — owned by banks. The highest-stakes surface in the system.
When the RBI cancelled Paytm Payments Bank's licence, Paytm had to ride Axis & Yes Bank rails overnight. The regulated trust layer — not the app — is the bet.

Part Three — The BetHarsh D

Design here is immature — and that's the opening.

Fewer than 5% of leaders can make objective design decisions. In banking, fewer still — so an early designer sets the standard.
Source · McKinsey, The Business Value of Design, 2018
Banks are design-maturing, not design-mature: in-house academies, new UX teams, real budgets arriving.
Opportunity — and risk
Low maturity means you can shape the function. It also means chaos: the team may not exist as you'd want it yet.
This is the honest version of "you're early" — leverage, not a guarantee.

The Bet · Honest TradeoffsHarsh D

What you're trading away — say it out loud.

Slow shipping
Release trains and change-advisory gates. Nothing ships in a weekend.
Compliance gating
Regulation sits upstream of design. Some doors are closed before you start.
Service-desk risk
Low maturity can mean design-as-decoration — until you fight for a seat.
In return: population-scale impact, stable formal employment, and the most consequential problems in the country. Fits if you want scale and stakes; if you want rapid shipping and end-to-end ownership, a product startup may suit you better.
03 · The Bet · choose with eyes open
The Bet · The WorkHarsh D

The highest-stakes canvas in the country.

Clarity over cleverness, error-prevention over delight, trust over engagement metrics — because a confusing flow here isn't a bounce, it's real harm. These are banking-heavy problems, concentrated in — not exclusive to — banks:
1
Bring the next 100M online
First-time digital users graduating from cash and feature phones.
2
Design for India's real conditions
A dozen languages, low literacy, ₹8,000 phones, patchy networks.
3
Make high-stakes feel simple
A loan, a dispute, a KYC check — safe and clear in a few taps.
4
Turn regulation into craft
Hard constraints that sharpen design — accessibility, performance budgets.

Act · The CTAHarsh D

Don't worship the logo. Vet the team.

Interview three places you'd normally dismiss — including a bank — and score each on the five criteria. Ask:
1
Who owns product decisions?
2
How many designers & researchers are on the team?
3
What shipped in the last 12 months?
4
How is design measured?
5
Which user problems are highest-stakes?
6
How much influence does design have past the mockup stage?
