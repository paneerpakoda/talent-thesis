Part Three · The BetHarsh D

Design in banks is still being built.

Decision rights
Fewer than 5% of companies make design decisions well. In banking, even fewer.

The opening
You don't join a finished design org. You help define one, and your standards become the standard.
The catch
The team, research, and craft may not exist yet. You'll have to build the case for design.
That's "you're early" told honestly: real leverage, not a guarantee.

The Bet · Honest TradeoffsHarsh D

What you trade away. Said out loud.

Slow shipping
Release trains and change-advisory gates. Nothing ships in a weekend.
Compliance gating
Regulation sits upstream of design. Some doors are closed before you start.
Service-desk risk
Low maturity can mean design-as-decoration, until you fight for a seat.
In return: population-scale impact, stable formal employment, and the most consequential problems in the country. Fits if you want scale and stakes. If you want rapid shipping and end-to-end ownership, a product startup may suit you better.
03 · The Bet · choose with eyes open
The Bet · The WorkHarsh D

The highest-stakes canvas in the country.

Learning velocity
Clarity over cleverness, error-prevention over delight, trust over engagement metrics. A confusing flow here isn't a bounce, it's real harm.
Bring the next 100M online
First-time digital users graduating from cash and feature phones.
Design for India's real conditions
A dozen languages, low literacy, ₹8,000 phones, patchy networks.
Make high-stakes feel simple
A loan, a dispute, a KYC check. Safe and clear in a few taps.
Turn regulation into craft
Hard constraints that sharpen design: accessibility, performance budgets.
